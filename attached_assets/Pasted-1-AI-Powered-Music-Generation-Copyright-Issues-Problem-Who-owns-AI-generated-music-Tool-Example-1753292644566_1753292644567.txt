1. AI-Powered Music Generation & Copyright Issues
Problem: Who owns AI-generated music?
Tool Example:

Boomy – https://boomy.com
Lets anyone create and publish AI-generated music in minutes. Raises copyright challenges for artists and platforms.
Class Activity: Discuss whether Boomy songs deserve copyright protection and how royalties should be handled.

2. Deepfake Voice & Licensing
Problem: AI can clone voices, creating legal and ethical dilemmas.
Tool Example:

Voicemod AI Voices – https://www.voicemod.net/ai-voices/

ElevenLabs – https://www.elevenlabs.io/
Clone any voice for song vocals, radio, or narration. Used both for authorised and controversial deepfakes.
Use Case: Grimes lets people use her AI voice model and profit-share via Elf.Tech https://elf.tech

3. Royalty Tracking & Transparent Payments
Problem: Artists don’t trust streaming royalty calculations.
Tool Example:

Revelator – https://www.revelator.com
A music rights and royalty platform that uses blockchain and smart contracts to automate royalty splits.

BeatBread – https://www.beatbread.com
Offers advances to artists based on streaming data — AI determines funding risk.
Activity: Analyse how smart contracts might replace traditional label accounting.

4. AI for A&R Discovery
Problem: Labels now use data instead of talent scouts.
Tool Example:

Chartmetric – https://www.chartmetric.com
AI tracks engagement across Spotify, TikTok, Instagram, etc., to find emerging artists.

Sodatone (by Warner Music) – https://sodatone.com
Machine learning for scouting unsigned talent using fanbase growth & streaming trends.
Student Task: Use Chartmetric to shortlist unsigned viral artists.

5. Personalised Recommendations & Discovery Bias
Problem: Recommendation engines promote already-popular artists, not new voices.
Tool Example:

Endlesss – https://endlesss.fm
AI-powered collaborative music platform for community-led music discovery.

Mubert – https://mubert.com
AI-generated music tailored to user behaviour — also powers background tracks for apps.
Debate Prompt: Should platforms be required to reveal how AI curates recommendations?

6. AI-Driven Data Scraping and Playlist Analysis
Problem: Scraping user playlists for training data is legally murky.
Tool Example:

Soundcharts – https://soundcharts.com
Tracks radio airplay, charts, and playlists globally. Scrapes data, but via licensing.

Musixmatch AI – https://about.musixmatch.com/
Provides AI-generated lyrics, translations, and metadata—licensed by Spotify and others.
Discussion: Where’s the line between “public data” and “protected data”?

7. IP Protection in the Metaverse
Problem: Virtual concerts and NFTs open up new IP risks.
Tool Example:

Async Music (by Async Art) – https://async.art/music
Artists can mint multi-layered, programmable music NFTs.

Arianee – https://www.arianee.org
A blockchain-based digital certificate for ownership and rights management.
Use Case: Show Travis Scott’s Fortnite concert and discuss how licensing differs from live gigs.

8. Privacy Laws & Listener Data
Problem: GDPR/CCPA restrict how platforms use listener behaviour data.
Tool Example:

Didomi Consent Platform – https://www.didomi.io/
AI-based compliance tool for user consent management across global privacy laws.
Group Activity: Draft a user data policy for a fictional AI streaming platform.

Bonus Topic: AI for Music Education & DIY Distribution
Tool Example:

Loudly – https://loudly.com
AI music creator for social and commercial use.

Amper Music (by Shutterstock) – https://www.shutterstock.com/discover/ai-music
Used by content creators for royalty-free, AI-generated soundtracks.
Homework: Try generating a track and explore upload options via DistroKid, RouteNote, or Soundrop.
